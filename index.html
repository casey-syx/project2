Polar Art Design Team

Design Visual Graphics @ Polar Art Studio

Polar Art Studio is a pottery workshop and art studio located in Markham, ON. As the sole designer on the Polar Art Design Team, I created infographics, posters, class schedules, and marketing campaigns to establish the brand identity and support the company's growth.

Sample Work

Most illustrations are created on digital drawing tablets, with layout and design details crafted in Figma and Adobe Illustrator. The overall visual communicates comfort, clarity, and simplicity. The colour palette is soft and pastel, chosen to create a welcoming atmosphere that resonates with both younger and older audiences. Since the primary target audience is Chinese-speaking, most text is written in Chinese. These designs have been printed and are prominently displayed in the studio.

Cross-Functional Team Collaboration

For our 2023 Mother's Day promotion, I collaborated with the marketing team at W Floral , a flower studio specializing in crafting boutique floral arrangements for various occasions. Our goal was to create social media campaigns that would encourage potential customers to choose our pottery workshop over other studios. Through multi-channel marketing exposure, we successfully attracted first-time customers and increased the returning rate of our existing customer base.

Key Takeaways

Balancing Visual Design with Message Clarity
 While captivating visuals are essential for grabbing users' attention, they should not come at the expense of clarity. It's easy to get carried away with design elements, but prioritizing message clarity helps maintain focus and prevents users from feeling overwhelmed or distracted.

 Building the Habit of Asking “ Why” Behind Every Element
 One crucial lesson I’ve learned is that marketing design is not simply about making things look attractive — it’s about designing with a purpose. Each element within a design should serve a specific function. To foster this mindset, it is a good habit to ask why each part of the design exists: Does it support the message? Is it essential? This approach not only prevents clutter but also ensures each component guides the users toward the desired outcome.

 Designing for the Target Audience
 Creating designs that resonate with the target audience is key to effective communication. When working on infographics for art classes aimed at children, I tailored the visual style to be child-friendly, using themes and colours that convey warmth and approachability. This helped create a welcoming atmosphere that appeals to young learners, making the content more relatable and engaging.
